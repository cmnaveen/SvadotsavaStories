> **Colour:** vibrant, saturated, harmonious — saffron, marigold gold, turquoise, rose-magenta, royal crimson, forest green and deep indigo.
> **Skin tones:** Only **Rama** (and, where they appear, divine forms of **Vishnu** and **Shiva**) has gentle **blue/teal skin**; **every other character has a natural human skin tone** — warm golden-brown, fair or tawny — and is **never blue**. *Vanaras* (monkey-people) keep natural fur colours.
> **Costume:** strictly **ancient, traditional Indian attire** — *dhotis*, *angavastrams*, draped *saris*, *mukut* crowns and traditional gold jewelry, bark-cloth and deerskin for forest-exiles, classical robes and armour — with **absolutely no modern clothing, fabrics, footwear or accessories**.
> **Composition:** dynamic, **full-bleed, edge-to-edge**, **absolutely no border and no frame**. Aspect ratio 4:3 (use portrait 3:4 for a cover).

## 👑 Character Reference Sheet (keep consistent on every page)
*(Rama, Sita and Lakshmana begin in royal palace attire; from the day they leave for exile they wear simple ascetic bark-cloth and saffron, with hair in ascetic top-knots, keeping their noble bearing.)*
- **Rama** — gentle prince, soft **blue/teal skin**, large kind eyes, calm half-smile; royal golden-yellow silk and jeweled crown at first, then simple saffron/bark-cloth and an ascetic top-knot in exile; carries a great bow. Serene and dutiful.
- **Sita** — radiant golden-brown complexion, jasmine in a long dark braid; rich red-and-gold royal sari, then a simple graceful ochre forest sari; gentle, loving, devoted.
- **Lakshmana** — fair golden-brown skin, alert and protective; saffron attire, bow always ready; ever at Rama's side.
- **Dasaratha** — aged noble king, grey beard, gentle eyes; cream-and-gold robes and a tall jeweled crown; often shown heartbroken.
- **Bharata** — Rama's noble younger brother (Kaikeyi's son), golden-brown skin, gentle earnest face; royal robes, later simple ascetic dress as a grieving, devoted regent.
- **Shatrughna** — the youngest brother, loyal companion of Bharata, princely attire.
- **Kaikeyi** — a beautiful young queen in fine silks and gold jewelry, proud and misled, her face torn between desire and doubt.
- **Manthara** — the hunchbacked maid, wizened and cunning with sharp scheming eyes, plain dark-red garments; sinister but kid-friendly, never grotesque.
- **Kaushalya** — Rama's gentle aged mother-queen, sorrowful kind face, elegant pale silks.
- **Sumantra** — the loyal old charioteer-minister, white beard, warm faithful face, simple noble dress.
- **Guha** — the kindly forest-tribe chief and boatman, sturdy and friendly, simple earthy garments, a small boat.
