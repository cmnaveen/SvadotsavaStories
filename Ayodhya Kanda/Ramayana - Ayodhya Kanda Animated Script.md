## DIRECTOR'S PRODUCTION NOTES — AYODHYA KANDA

The Ayodhya Kanda is the emotional heart of the entire Ramayana — the longest and most complex kanda in Valmiki's text, containing roughly 4,000 shlokas. Where the Bala Kanda was adventure and wonder, this kanda is built on a different architecture: it is a profound meditation on dharma, sacrifice, and devotion. The visual language must shift accordingly.

**Tone shift:** Warm, festive gold at the opening transitions to a deep, aching blue-grey once Manthara acts. Forest scenes at Chitrakuta are vibrantly green and alive — a visual paradox that underlines Rama's serenity against the city's grief. Ayodhya in mourning uses near-total darkness and cold moonlight.

**New characters this kanda:** Kaikeyi, Manthara, Bharata, Guha, Sumantra, Kaushalya (now foregrounded), and the spirit-presence of the unseen Shravan Kumar.

**Scriptural discipline rules for this kanda:**
1. Kaikeyi is NOT portrayed as simply evil. Valmiki gives her genuine dignity even in her error. Her face should show conviction, not malice — she truly believes she is protecting her son.
2. Bharata is NEVER villainous or complicit. He is as shocked and heartbroken as anyone. His scene with Kaikeyi must show grief, not menace.
3. Rama NEVER shows grief, self-pity, or anger about the exile. He is serene throughout. This serenity is not cold — it is the most extraordinary human warmth dressed in total dharmic composure.
4. Dasaratha is not weak. He is a man trapped between two absolute dharmic obligations — his royal oath given in battle-thanks and his love for his son. Both are sacred. He is shattered, not a coward.
5. The Shravan Kumar story is strictly the Valmiki version (Sarga 63): Dasaratha, hunting as a young prince, shoots a sound-aimed arrow (shabda-bhedi) at a sound he mistakes for an elephant drinking. The young ascetic Shravan Kumar falls. His blind parents give the curse before dying of grief.

## MASTER VISUAL STYLE GUIDE

All prompts in this document share the following base style. Paste these lines at the end of every scene prompt:

> **Animation Style:** 3D animated cinematic style, vibrant colors, Pixar-esque aesthetics, fluid character movement, kid-friendly, 24fps.
> **Lighting:** Warm cinematic directional light, gentle golden rim-light, soft ambient glow. Scene-specific lighting specified per prompt.
> **Skin Tones:** ONLY Rama (and divine Vishnu/Shiva forms in celestial visions) has luminous blue/teal skin. Every other character has a natural human skin tone — warm golden-brown, fair, tawny, or deep brown.
> **Costume:** Strictly ancient traditional Indian attire — silk dhotis, angavastrams, draped saris, mukuta crowns, traditional gold jewelry, bark-cloth for forest exiles, classical armour. Absolutely no modern clothing.
> **Composition:** Dynamic full-bleed, edge-to-edge. No border, no frame. Rich painterly backgrounds.
