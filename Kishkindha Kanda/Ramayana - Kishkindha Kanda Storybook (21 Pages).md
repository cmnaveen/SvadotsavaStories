> **Lighting:** warm **cinematic** directional light with a gentle glowing rim-light and soft ambient glow.
> **Colour:** vibrant, saturated, harmonious — forest greens, saffron, marigold gold, turquoise, rose-magenta and deep indigo.
> **Skin tones:** Only **Rama** (and, where they appear, divine forms of **Vishnu** and **Shiva**) has gentle **blue/teal skin**; **every other character has a natural human skin tone** — warm golden-brown, fair or tawny — and is **never blue**. *Vanaras* (monkey-people) keep natural fur colours.
> **Costume:** strictly **ancient, traditional Indian attire** — *dhotis*, *angavastrams*, draped *saris*, *mukut* crowns and traditional gold jewelry, bark-cloth and deerskin for forest-exiles, classical robes and armour — with **absolutely no modern clothing, fabrics, footwear or accessories**.
> **Composition:** dynamic, **full-bleed, edge-to-edge**, **absolutely no border and no frame**. Aspect ratio 4:3 (use portrait 3:4 for a cover).

## 👑 Character Reference Sheet (keep consistent on every page)
*(Rama and Lakshmana are forest-exiles in simple saffron/bark-cloth with ascetic top-knots. The vanaras are noble, friendly monkey-people, semi-stylised and heroic — not cartoonish.)*
- **Rama** — gentle prince, soft **blue/teal skin**, large kind eyes, calm brave half-smile; simple saffron/bark-cloth wrap, ascetic top-knot, golden quiver and great bow. Serene, sorrowful for lost Sita yet noble.
- **Lakshmana** — fair golden-brown skin, alert and protective; saffron bark-cloth, bow ready; ever at Rama's side.
- **Hanuman** — the mighty, devoted monkey-hero; golden-furred *vanara* with a kind, noble, intelligent face, expressive eyes, a long tail, a simple loincloth and a few gold ornaments; humble, strong and loyal.
- **Sugriva** — the exiled monkey-king; tawny-brown fur, an open, earnest, sometimes worried face, royal beads and a simple crown when enthroned.
- **Bali (Vali)** — Sugriva's mighty elder brother, the powerful golden-bronze monkey-king of Kishkindha; large, regal and fierce, a golden chain-garland, a proud face — imposing but not gory.
- **Tara** — Bali's wise and gentle queen, a graceful *vanari* with soft eyes and elegant ornaments; thoughtful and dignified.
- **Angada** — Bali's brave young son, a strong noble young *vanara* prince with gold armlets.
- **Jambavan** — the ancient, wise king of the bears; a great venerable bear with kind knowing eyes and a calm, grandfatherly presence.
- **Sampati** — a colossal aged eagle/vulture (Jatayu's brother), broad russet-gold wings, wise eyes.
