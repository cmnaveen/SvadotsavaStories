## How to Use This Book
Each page has two parts:

## 🎨 Master Style Block (for visual consistency)
> **Medium & style:** Richly detailed **digital children's-storybook illustration**, in the style of a modern *Illustrated Ramayana for Children* cover — **semi-realistic yet softly stylized** characters with expressive friendly faces, large warm eyes and smooth skin; polished **digital painting** with gentle **cel-shading**, soft airbrushed gradients and crisp clean highlights; finely detailed costumes, fur, ornaments, armour and architecture; **lush painterly backgrounds** with atmospheric depth and soft focus.
> **Lighting:** warm **cinematic** directional light with a gentle glowing rim-light and soft ambient glow.
> **Colour:** vibrant, saturated, harmonious — ocean turquoise, saffron, marigold gold, royal crimson, forest green and deep indigo.
> **Skin tones:** Only **Rama** (and, where they appear, divine forms of **Vishnu** and **Shiva**) has gentle **blue/teal skin**; **every other character has a natural human skin tone** — warm golden-brown, fair or tawny — and is **never blue**. *Vanaras* (monkey-people) keep natural fur colours; *rakshasas* (titans) are imposing but natural-toned.
> **Costume:** strictly **ancient, traditional Indian attire** — *dhotis*, *angavastrams*, draped *saris*, *mukut* crowns and traditional gold jewelry, bark-cloth and deerskin for forest-exiles, classical warrior armour — with **absolutely no modern clothing, fabrics, footwear or accessories**.
> **Composition:** dynamic, **full-bleed, edge-to-edge**, **absolutely no border and no frame**. Aspect ratio 4:3 (use portrait 3:4 for a cover).

## 👑 Character Reference Sheet (keep consistent on every page)
- **Rama** — gentle prince-hero, soft **blue/teal skin**, large kind eyes, calm resolute face; simple saffron/bark-cloth of a forest-exile with an ascetic top-knot, a great bow and quiver; noble and serene even in battle. (Wears royal silks and a golden crown only at the final coronation.)
- **Lakshmana** — fair golden-brown skin, devoted and brave; saffron bark-cloth, bow ever ready; at Rama's side.
- **Sita** — radiant golden-brown complexion, jasmine in a long braid, simple ochre-and-saffron sari, minimal gold jewelry; gentle, faithful and dignified.
- **Hanuman** — the mighty golden-furred *vanara* hero, kind noble face, long tail, simple loincloth and a few gold ornaments; humble, immensely strong, can grow giant; a soft heroic glow.
- **Sugriva** — the tawny monkey-king of Kishkindha, earnest and brave, simple gold crown and beads.
- **Vibhishana** — Ravana's good and noble younger brother, a dignified righteous *rakshasa* with natural skin, kind wise face, fine but gentle robes; later King of Lanka.
- **Angada** — brave young *vanara* prince, gold armlets. **Jambavan** — the ancient wise bear-king. **Nala** — the clever *vanara* builder of the bridge.
- **Ravana** — mighty **ten-headed demon-king of Lanka**, natural dark complexion, ten crowned heads (one dominant, others fanned behind), many arms, ornate golden armour and crimson robes, fierce eyes; imposing and regal but **never gory**.
- **Kumbhakarna** — Ravana's colossal sleeping giant-brother, mountain-sized, natural-toned, a sleepy then fierce face; huge but kept kid-friendly.
- **Indrajit (Meghanada)** — Ravana's powerful warrior-son, master of illusion, handsome fierce face, ornate armour, often wreathed in magical mist.
- **Mandodari** — Ravana's noble, gentle queen; graceful, sorrowful, in fine ancient royal attire.
